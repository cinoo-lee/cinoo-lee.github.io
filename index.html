About

I'm a Senior Applied Scientist at Microsoft, working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and social psychology.

I completed my Ph.D. in Social Psychology at Stanford University, mentored by Jennifer Eberhardt, Hazel Markus, and Dan Jurafsky, and was a postdoctoral fellow at Stanford SPARQ, a behavioral science "do-tank." Through SPARQ, I partnered with Nextdoor for six years on research that helped reduce platform incivility. Before that, I earned my B.A. in Psychology with Honors from Rice University.

My work has been featured in NPR, MIT Technology Review, The Guardian, and Business Insider.
