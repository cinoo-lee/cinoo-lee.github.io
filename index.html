About

I'm a Senior Applied Scientist on an incubation team at Microsoft, where I apply behavioral science to build more human-centered AI.

I completed my Ph.D. in Psychology at Stanford University, mentored by Jennifer Eberhardt, Hazel Markus, and Dan Jurafsky, and was a postdoctoral fellow at Stanford SPARQ, a behavioral science "do-tank." Through SPARQ, I partnered with Nextdoor on research that helped reduce platform incivility. Before that, I earned my B.A. in Psychology with Honors from Rice University.

My research has been supported by the Russell Sage Foundation and featured in media such as The New York Times, The Economist, NPR, TIME, and The Guardian.
